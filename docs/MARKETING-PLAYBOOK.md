# DataSpark — Social Media Marketing Playbook

## Strategy Overview

**Goal:** Build a waitlist of 5,000+ data science students before launch
**Timeline:** 8-12 weeks pre-launch
**Budget:** $0 (organic-first, paid later)
**Core channels:** TikTok, Instagram Reels, LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Snapchat

---

## Brand Voice & Aesthetic

### Voice
- Confident but not arrogant — "we built what we wished existed"
- Direct, no fluff — matches data science culture
- Slightly provocative — challenge the status quo ("StrataScratch teaches you syntax. We teach you to think.")
- Relatable — "we've been in those interviews, we know what they actually ask"

### Visual Identity
- Dark theme (matches the product)
- Accent colors: electric blue (#0EA5E9), purple (#8B5CF6), amber (#F59E0B)
- Code-style typography for emphasis
- Clean, minimal, professional — NOT "influencer aesthetic"
- Screen recordings of the actual product in action

## Content Pillars (5 types of content, rotate daily)

### Pillar 1: "The Interview Gap" (Problem Awareness)
**Format:** Faceless slideshows, text-on-screen TikToks
**Purpose:** Make people feel the pain of current prep tools

Example posts:
- "POV: You studied SQL for 3 months on StrataScratch and then the interviewer asks 'our conversion dropped 25%, walk me through how you'd investigate it'"
- "Things that actually come up in data science interviews that no platform prepares you for" → slideshow: system design, stakeholder communication, ambiguity, AI tooling
- "I got rejected from 5 DS interviews. It wasn't my SQL. It was everything around it."
- "What bootcamps teach you vs what interviews actually test" → side by side comparison
- "The PM says your A/B test is significant at p=0.049. She ran 12 tests. What do you say?" → cliffhanger → "We teach you exactly this"

### Pillar 2: "Quick Concept Explainer" (Value-First Content)
**Format:** 30-60 second faceless videos with animated visuals, carousels
**Purpose:** Teach something genuinely useful — builds trust and follows

Example posts:
- "Bias vs Variance in 30 seconds" → use our actual visualization as the visual
- "SQL Window Functions explained with one diagram" → clean animated graphic
- "What a p-value ACTUALLY means (it's not what you think)" → text reveal
- "The 5 SQL patterns that appear in 80% of DS interviews" → carousel
- "Gradient Descent explained like you're 10" → animation from our platform
- "How Netflix's recommendation system actually works (simplified)" → architecture diagram

### Pillar 3: "Day in the Life / Behind the Build" (Authenticity)
**Format:** Quick selfie videos, screen recordings, build logs
**Purpose:** Show the humans behind the product, build community

Example posts:
- Screen recording of building a visualization: "Today I animated gradient descent from scratch"
- "We just wrote our 200th practice question. Here's what we've learned about what interviews actually test"
- "Building an AI tutor that only answers questions about statistics. Here's why scope matters."
- Timelapse of the platform evolving from V1 → V2

### Pillar 4: "Interview Scenarios" (Engagement Hooks)
**Format:** Interactive posts, polls, "what would you do?" scenarios
**Purpose:** Highest engagement — people love testing themselves

Example posts:
- "Your model has 99% accuracy in dev, 61% in production. What happened?" → comments section explodes
- "The CEO says 'can't we just eliminate both Type I and Type II errors?' What do you say?" → engagement bait
- "You find that Drug A wins overall but Drug B wins in every subgroup. Ship which one?" → Simpson's paradox
- Poll: "Which SQL concept trips you up most?" → Window functions / Recursive CTEs / Indexing / JOINs
- "Rate this SQL query 1-10" → show a bad query → people comment corrections → post the fixed version next day

### Pillar 5: "Social Proof & Results" (Post-Launch)
**Format:** Testimonials, stats, success stories
**Purpose:** Convert followers to users

Example posts:
- "User went from bombing interviews to getting offers after 6 weeks on DataSpark"
- "Most completed question this week: 'Diagnose a Metric Drop at DoorDash' — here's what 80% of people missed"
- Waitlist counter milestones: "1,000 people signed up in 48 hours"

## Platform-Specific Strategy

### TikTok (Primary — highest reach for educational content)

**Posting schedule:** 1-2 per day
**Best formats:**
1. **Faceless slideshows** with trending audio — "Things data science bootcamps don't teach you"
2. **Screen recordings** of the platform's visualizations in action (these are inherently satisfying to watch)
3. **Text-on-screen** concept explainers with dramatic reveals
4. **"POV" format** — "POV: The interviewer asks about system design and you only studied LeetCode"

**Hashtags:** #datascience #dataanalytics #techinterviews #learntocode #datascienceinterview #pythonprogramming #sqlquery #machinelearning #techjobs #dataspark

**Hook patterns (first 2 seconds):**
- "Stop studying SQL syntax if you want a DS job"
- "This one concept separates junior from senior data scientists"
- "I've interviewed 200 DS candidates. Here's why most fail."
- "The question that 90% of bootcamp grads can't answer"
- "This is what a real DS interview looks like (it's not LeetCode)"

### Instagram Reels + Carousels

**Posting schedule:** 1 reel + 2 carousels per week
**Best formats:**
1. **Carousels** — "5 SQL patterns for interviews" (swipeable, saveable, shareable)
2. **Reels** — same content as TikTok, cross-posted
3. **Stories** — daily behind-the-scenes, polls, "test yourself" questions

**Carousel templates:**
- Slide 1: Bold hook question
- Slides 2-8: Each point with clean visual
- Slide 9: "Follow for more" + "Join the waitlist — link in bio"

### LinkedIn (Professional audience — highest conversion to waitlist)

**Posting schedule:** 3-4 text posts per week
**Best formats:**
1. **Long-form text posts** — "I've interviewed 200 data scientists. Here's the #1 reason they fail (it's not technical skills)"
2. **Contrarian takes** — "Hot take: StrataScratch is making data scientists WORSE at their jobs"
3. **Build in public** — "Week 4 of building DataSpark. Here's what I learned about how interviews actually work"
4. **Polls** — "What's the hardest part of DS interviews?" → builds engagement + market research

**LinkedIn is where waitlist conversions happen.** Every post ends with a soft CTA: "We're building something for this. Link in comments."

### Twitter/X

**Posting schedule:** 1-2 per day (short, punchy)
**Best formats:**
1. **Hot takes** — "Unpopular opinion: You don't need to know how to invert a binary tree to be a great data scientist"
2. **Threads** — "Here's how to answer 'diagnose this metric drop' in a DS interview (thread 🧵)"
3. **Quick tips** — one SQL trick, one Python trick, one stats concept per day
4. **Build in public** — screenshot of code, progress updates

### Snapchat Stories

**Posting schedule:** 3-5 stories per day during active building
**Best formats:**
1. **Quick tips** with text overlay on dark background (matches brand)
2. **"Did you know?"** stats facts — "Did you know p=0.049 with 12 tests is basically random?"
3. **Countdown to launch** — daily stories building anticipation
4. **Behind the scenes** — quick phone recordings of building, designing, writing questions

## Waitlist Strategy

### Landing Page Requirements
- One-page, dark theme, matches product aesthetic
- Animated visualization running in the background (the normal distribution or gradient descent)
- Clear value prop: "StrataScratch teaches syntax. DataSpark teaches thinking."
- Email capture form
- Social proof: "Join 2,000+ data scientists" (update as it grows)
- What's coming: quick preview of features
- No pricing — just "Get early access"

### Email Sequence (Post-Signup)
1. **Immediately:** "You're in. Here's what we're building." (product preview)
2. **Day 3:** Free value — "5 interview questions that separate good from great" (PDF or link)
3. **Day 7:** Behind the scenes — "Here's a sneak peek of the platform"
4. **Day 14:** Community — "Join our Discord / Slack for early feedback"
5. **Day 21:** Urgency — "We're launching in X weeks. Early access gets 50% off"
6. **Launch day:** "We're live. You get in first."

### Referral Mechanic
- "Refer 3 friends → get 1 month free"
- "Move up the waitlist" — share your unique link
- Leaderboard: top referrers get lifetime access

## Growth Hacks

### 1. Comment Strategy
- Comment thoughtfully on EVERY viral data science post on TikTok/LinkedIn
- Not "check out my product" — genuinely helpful comments that establish expertise
- Pin your best comment on your own videos

### 2. Duets / Stitches (TikTok)
- Stitch viral DS interview videos: "Here's what they should have said..."
- Duet coding tutorial videos: "This works, but here's the production-ready version"

### 3. Collaborations
- DM 10 data science micro-influencers (5K-50K followers)
- Offer early access in exchange for a review
- Target: data science bootcamp instructors, career coaches, content creators

### 4. Reddit / Discord Seeding
- Post genuinely helpful content in r/datascience, r/learnpython, r/SQL
- Don't shill — provide value first, mention DataSpark only when directly relevant
- Join data science Discord servers, answer questions, build reputation

### 5. Free Tools as Lead Magnets
- "DataSpark SQL Cheat Sheet" — downloadable PDF, requires email
- "50 Interview Questions That Actually Get Asked" — PDF guide
- "A/B Test Calculator" — free web tool, has DataSpark branding + waitlist CTA
- "Data Science Interview Readiness Quiz" — 10 questions, shows your score + "improve with DataSpark"
